Service Trees: Exploring Dependencies and Relationships
Have you ever stopped to think about the number and types of components that business services in your organization depend on ? How are you monitoring the set of relationships and dependencies between business processes, and instances, containers, applications, microservices and dozens of other cloud or on-premise components ? How are you defining, for example, the real impact on a specific service after a message queue contention or an increase of I/O load in a given instance ?
In fact, there is a lack of tools on the market that can visually display the status of service's components in real time, and at the same time show dependencies and relationships between these components.

ServiceXplorer  creates service maps using a relationship structure based on the depend ency between the most diverse component items composing these services, establishing how the incident severities from these components impact the availability and the service performance. The solution provides agile diagnosis and visualization in the location of root causes of problems. You can manually create service maps, or pro grammatically perform POST operations using ServiceXplorer's REST APIs, thus allowing the use of CMDB tools as sources of information.

In  addition to the current hierarchica​l model to establish dependency relationships between components, ServiceXplorer will support a mesh model in its next releases, very similar to what we find in BPM modeling.

DDM
DDM is an agentless tool for comprehensive discovery and dependency mapping solution, able to handle multi-cloud environments along with on-premises machines, spanning across multiple environments so that administrators have a complete picture of the IT estate, and at the same time allowing to maintain inventory of all their configuration management databases (CMDB). It also gives them the ability to understand the impact of changes to resources and business. DDM will be included as a module for MonaaS project and can also be used as a source of information for ServiceXplorer.

MonaaS - Monitoring as a Service
MonaaS is an online monitoring service​ of infrastructure, application and services with automatic execution of actions and generation of notifications, reports and dashboards.  MonaaS also performs automatic discovery of resources, and applications distributed in your environment, allowing the user to select from the set, the key components to be monitored.
 MonaaS has its core based on ELK (Elastic, Logstash and Kibana), and control processes handling mechanisms of security and integrating all architecture components that can be in cloud, on-premise or distributed in a hybrid environment. The solution also provides mechanisms of high scalability and a se​t of APIs to programmatically configure, query, and manage the monitoring environment.

K4TPA  - Kafka for Transaction Packets Analysis
 The idea in this case is to gather information from network probes and port mirroring for traffic analysis, error detection and financial transaction statistics. Basically, the solution is based on frameworks from the Kafka ecosystem, using Kafka Source Connectors to capture events from these external sources, Kafka Sink Connectors to consume the streams generated by processing data through ksqlDB and Kafka Streams. In addition to the security control issues, this solution aims to ensure high availability of all component items present in the pipeline, and proactively trigger corrective actions capable of maintaining the functionality of the environment and meeting the low latency requirements of high volume streams. .
