Service Trees: Exploring Dependencies and Relationships

Have you ever stopped to think about the number and types of components that business services in your organization depend on ? How are you monitoring the set of relationships and dependencies between business processes, and instances, containers, applications, microservices and dozens of other cloud or on-premise components ? How to define, for example, the real impact on a specific service after an increase of I/O load in a given instance ?
In fact, there is a lack of tools on the market that can visually display the status of service's components in real time, and at the same time show dependencies and relationships between these components.

ServiceXplorer creates service maps using a relationship structure based on the dependency between the most diverse component items composing these services, establishing how the incident severities from these components impact the availability and the service performance. The solution provides agile diagnosis and visualization in the location of root causes of problems.

MonaaS - Monitoring as a Service

MonaaS is an online monitoring service of infrastructure, application and services with automatic execution of actions and generation of notifications, reports and dashboards.
MonaaS has its core based on ELK (Elastic, Logstash and Kibana), and control processes handling mechanisms of security and integrating all architecture components that can be in cloud, on-premise or distributed in a hybrid environment.
The solution also provides mechanisms of high scalability and a set of APIs to programmatically configure, query, and manage the monitoring environment.
